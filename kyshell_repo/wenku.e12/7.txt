AS the industry advances,however,other suits may have an even greater
impact.Companies are unlikely to file many more patents for human DNA
molecules-most are already patented or in the public domain.firms are now studying
how genes interact,looking for correlations that might be used to determine the causes
of disease or predict a drug's efficacy.Companies are eager to win patents
for'connecting the dots',explains Hans Sauer,a lawyer for the BIO.
